**One-paragraph explanation:**

> Before Meseret's explainer, the model card claimed that SimPO training was the safety lift on tone compliance, with the deterministic anti-marker filtering in [`scoring_evaluator.py`](../../sales-agent-evaluation-bench/scoring_evaluator.py) cited as a backstop. I had no way to defend, from inspecting the LoRA adapter, whether the adapter itself was doing token-localized tone-violation detection or whether it had absorbed a body-shape correlation that the dev partition (same prospect framing, same length envelope as train) was hiding. After understanding the per-token decomposition of the length-normalized SimPO loss — that each token contributes 1/|y| of the gradient and that tokens following the first discriminative position accumulate context-divergence smear — I wired Meseret's mask-and-rescore probe into `training/eval_dev.py` as a new evaluation pass. The probe borrows [`scoring_evaluator.py`](../../sales-agent-evaluation-bench/scoring_evaluator.py)'s banned-phrase list as the discriminative-token oracle, computes the SimPO reward gap on 20 held-out near-identical pairs both intact and with discriminative phrases masked, and reports the survival ratio. On the shipped adapter the survival ratio came back at **34%** — solidly in the "mixed: partial smear, body shape contributes but is not dominant" band. That number is the load-bearing fact: the adapter is not a clean token-localized detector, but it is also not a sequence-diffuse one — which means the v0.2 threaded-traces expansion will degrade the critic's calibration on novel body shapes, but probably not catastrophically. The model card's Tone-Preservation section now reports the 34% survival ratio with the verdict band and a one-line caveat that the deterministic anti-marker filter is doing more of the safety lift than the SimPO training alone, and the memo's tone-preservation paragraph is revised to make the joint claim ("the combination of SimPO training and deterministic anti-marker filtering improved tone compliance, and we cannot yet attribute the improvement cleanly to either component") instead of the cleaner-sounding but harder-to-defend single-component claim. The per-token attribution dump is also wired in as a debug-only output behind `--attribution-dump`, so when the v0.2 expansion lands and a draft starts mis-firing the rejection sampler, I can print the heatmap on the offending pair and see whether the smear has eaten the discriminative-token signal. The decision-memo paragraph that referenced the LoRA adapter's safety lift will be revised in a follow-up commit if the v0.2 evaluation shows the survival ratio drifting upward on the new body-shape distribution; the body-shape calibration question is separately tracked in `signoff.md` as the next adjacent gap.
